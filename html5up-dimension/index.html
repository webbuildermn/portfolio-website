Intro

Technology is going to change the world again and again and again, as it has done times before in the past. There is nothing better than harnessing new technology to make life better while also keeping balance with the eternal way of good natural living.

It is my joy to be a small part of this perennial technological revolution as a creator and developer and to facilitate opening up of new possiblities for individuals and organizations and for humanity.

Work

Upon graduating from University in 2004 I went abroad to teach English as a second language overseas, where I worked in places such as Japan, Poland, Turkey, Germany and the Czech Republic

Returning home for a few more years of education, I earned my CPA and made public accounting my new profession.

It was during this time that I realized accounting could not provide me with the challenge and satisfaction that I sought, but where I also learned some of the power of MS Excel and VBA as well as my interest in data, analytics and technology design, and in helping clients solve problems.

I was our firm's go to Excel expert and the only one that could really do anthing inside VBA. It was my passion and I built several large data scraping and report generating VBA based tools among other things. My largely disorganized repo of VBA code can be found here.Download the OneStopReorganized.xlam file for simplicity.

In addition to the custom macros and solutions I offered, for which I got kudos, I spent most evenings and weekends learning code in various languages through online self-paced courses.

I took various tutorials and online courses on youtube. For example I made a Django polls app and a sound synthesizer with the help of the legendary Javidx9 a.k.a. OneLoneCoder.

I took a large many month online course on Game Development with Unreal Engine in C++. While I never was pursuing the path of a game developer, this course taught me a lot about game devlopment and development in general, game engines and version control and C++ and OOP in general (Unreal uses a lot of classes!) as well as game physics and visual studio- the original, all of which I thought was very interesting. I have always been more interested in how games work under the hood than in actually playing them. You can see a few of my repos for this course here under Building Escape and Battle Tank game as well as the Bull Cow game

I also took a pretty large course in Python and Ethical Hacking, which also taught me a lot about Linux and Virtual Machines and I had started to look into machine learning and data science when I decided to go all in and begin my career-focused path of web development at Lambda School

I have built a few websites for clients and have done some freelance consulting work for profit on the side, but this is likely going to become a more significant part of my life moving forward as I get out of the employee mindset and have a chance to be entrepreneurial.

I have always been of an entrepreneurial mindset and by developing a real and in demand technical skillbase, I know I will be able to create for myself the kind of career I have passion for, with more opportunity to be creative and to scale things up.

About

My name is Gerald Ryan. I'm the owner of Technical Business Solutions, LLC based in Minneapolis, MN.

I have a background in fiannce and accounting and I have my CPA but my true passion is technology, particularily where it is a game changer.

In 2020 I left my job and the accounting profession to pursue a technology related career, enrolling in Lambda School's 9 month comprehensive Full Stack Web Development program.

Besides this, I have a passion for travel, exploring the world, meeting new people, music martial arts and ballroom dance.
